Which list was easiest to sort? Why?

If we aren't counting the already sorted list, which would be the easiest by default 
as no comparisons beyond the actual length of the list would be necessary, would be the random list.
The random list will be able to stop sooner than the reversed list as one of the numbers is already in
the right place, additionally, even if I didn't know the exact placement of the numbers in the random list,
the random list still has a higher chance of not needing as many comparisons as a list that is directly backwards
and will require the most amount of comparisons by default.

Which list required the most work?

The list that required the most work to sort is the reversed list as it is guaranteed
to have to compare each number to everything else in the list and everything before it
as well to ensure proper sorting has been done.

Why is sorting useful before searching?

Sorting keeps your lists orderly making the search more efficient by reducing 
the potential number of comparisons needed to find what is being searched for.

When might linear search still be useful?

Linear search is useful for short lists with not too many numbers too go through or
unsorted numbers as it is just searching through the values and not sorting and then doing the search.
